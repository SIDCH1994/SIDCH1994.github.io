About Me

I developed my interest in teaching through mathematics and statistics. During my graduate studies, I worked closely with students in statistics labs and saw how much difference clear explanation, patient support, and real-world examples can make. That experience grew further when I helped redesign a nine-lab statistics sequence to make learning more accessible, applied, and concept-focused.

My academic background is in Data Science, but much of my work has centered on statistics, probability, modeling, and quantitative reasoning. I am especially interested in helping students connect mathematics to real data, real decisions, and real careers.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe students learn mathematics best when they can connect ideas to meaning. My approach emphasizes concept understanding, interpretation, and real-world application rather than memorization alone. I aim to create a classroom environment where students feel supported, challenged, and encouraged to think critically.

I am especially interested in teaching mathematics through statistics and data. Topics such as probability, variation, inference, and modeling help students see that mathematics is not only about solving problems on paper, but also about understanding patterns, evidence, and decisions in the real world.
